Programs Offered

THE MASTERS PROGRAM

Students enrolled in the master’s degree have to finish a minimum of 37 units to earn the degree. It enables the student to learn the theories, principles, and/or concepts and integrate these into the skills that he/she desires to specialize into

  • Master in Community Development
  • Master in Community Health Development
  • Master in Cooperative Management
  • Master in Development Communication
  • Master in Human Resource Management
  • Master in Urban Management
  • Master in Non-Formal Education (Alternative Learning System)

POST-BACCALAUREATE

The Diploma Courses is a post-baccalaureate degree with 21 credit units. Students who complete the Diploma Course with passing performance shall be given a Certificate of Completion and may be admitted to the master’s degree if qualified.

  • Diploma in Urban Management (ladderized to a master’s program)
  • Diploma in Training Management

The Certificate Courses is a post-baccalaureate program with 12 credit units. Other pre baccalaureate certificate courses without credit units are also offered for specialized group of students.
Students who complete the course with passing performance shall be given a Certificate of Completion and may be admitted to the master’s program if qualified.

  • Certificate in Community Health Development (MLGP Program of DOH)
  • Certificate in Organic Agriculture
